App Concept

We have chosen Use Case C (Your Own).

The Problem: Keeping track of cards encountered during trades or gameplay is difficult. Players need a way to quickly "bookmark" cards they see in the wild without manually typing names.

The Solution: A scanner utility that instantly identifies cards via camera and adds them to a persistent history. Users can manage this list by "favoriting" key cards or deleting unwanted scans.

Note on Scope: We previously built a Deck Builder. This project focuses specifically on the Scanner & Collection Management workflow to demonstrate a clean offline-first CRUD architecture.

Target User:

  • Traders & Collectors: Who need to quickly log cards they are interested in.
  • Casual Players: Who want to "bookmark" cards they see opponents play for later research.

User Flow (CRUD)

The app implements a strict CRUD cycle using a local database:

  • Create: Scan a card via Camera -> Added to DB.
  • Read: View all scans in the History Screen.
  • Update: Tap the "Heart" icon to mark a card as a Favorite.
  • Delete: Remove a card from the history permanently.

Usability Test Plan

The goal is to evaluate the efficiency of the "Scanner-to-Collection" workflow. As an offline-first utility, the bridge between physical scanning and digital storage must be seamless.

View Heuristic Evaluation (Initial Prototype)

Method: Nielsen’s 10 Heuristics applied to the Jan 2026 Prototype.

Visibility of system status:

Positive: The "Result Overlay" gives instant feedback on a successful scan.

Negative: It is not always clear if the camera is actively "searching" or idle before a card is detected.

Match between system & real world:

Positive: The use of a "Heart" icon for favorites and "Trash" for delete matches standard mental models.

Negative: The term "History" implies a log, which might confuse users looking for a "Collection."

User control and freedom:

Positive: Users can easily dismiss the scan result overlay to try again.

Negative: There is currently no "Undo" option for accidental deletions.

Error prevention:

Negative (Critical): There is no confirmation dialog when deleting a card. This allows for fast cleanup but risks accidental data loss.

Help and documentation:

Negative: There is no onboarding or tutorial for first-time users.

Research Questions & Hypotheses

  • Efficiency: How quickly can a user scan a physical card and see it appear in their digital history?
  • CRUD Intuitiveness: Is the cycle of scanning (Create), viewing (Read), and favoriting (Update) intuitive without instructions?
  • Error Recovery: Can users easily recover from accidental scans?

Test Protocol (Tasks)

Task 1 (Create): "Use the app to scan this specific card and ensure it is saved." (Metric: Time to completion)

Task 2 (Update): "Find the card you just scanned in the history and mark it as a 'Favorite'." (Metric: Navigation accuracy)

Task 3 (Read): "Go to your Favorites collection and verify the card is there." (Metric: Success/Failure)

Task 4 (Delete): "Remove the card from your Favorites list." (Metric: Discovery of delete function)

We will visualize the results using Bar Charts for Time-on-Task and Line Charts for the SEQ (Single Ease Question) scores across the 4 tasks.
